Tech Evolution

How Technology
Transformed

Technology doesn't change in a straight line — it lurches, plateaus, and leaps. We document the defining moments that changed how we work, create, and think.

2024
Before

Manual parameter tuning: hours of calibration, dozens of failed prints, and tribal knowledge passed through forums.

→
After

AI-optimized settings per material and geometry. First-print success rates jumped from ~60% to 90%+ for experienced users.

The integration of machine learning into slicing software represents a fundamental shift: the printer is no longer just a physical device executing G-code — it's part of an intelligent system that learns from every layer. CodeDomeLabs documented 6 months of AI-assisted prints, logging success rates, material usage, and parameter evolution.

2022
Before

Single material, single color. Functional multi-material parts required assembly of separate prints, adding complexity and failure points.

→
After

Rigid + flexible in a single print. Functional hinges, gaskets, and grip surfaces — no assembly required.

Our lab documented the transition to multi-material printing across 18 months, tracking how the technology evolved from unreliable curiosity to production-quality capability. The key inflection: purge tower optimization and improved filament switching reduced material waste from 30% to under 8%.

2020
Before

Proprietary designs with locked ecosystems. Upgrades meant buying new machines. Community modifications were unsupported.

→
After

Fully open designs. Community modifications became official upgrades. Users became co-developers of the machines they depended on.

The open-source hardware movement fundamentally changed who drives 3D printing innovation. CodeDomeLabs built a Voron 2.4 from scratch and documented every choice — from frame to firmware — creating a reference guide used by over 2,000 builders.
